Healthy conflict

We’re usually thought to avoid conflict, but sometimes conflict is good when is properly handled. For instance, at work, were there’s a disagreement and people are invested in their suggestions, it means they care. It’s a little bit controversial, and shouldn’t be confused with being stubborn, but showing interest in something is a good sign.

Now, the way the conflict can be beneficial is that all the parties make an effort to understand each other. They don’t have to agree, just listed to the other part; What’s the motivation?, Ask questions, what-if scenarios. Once all the information is at hand the decision maker (usually the person responsible for the task) can make a call.

It might not be easy for the part that didn’t get their way, but it’s important to commit and move on, that puts end to the conflict and more important allows to execute and see the result. The decision might have been the wrong one, but as long as it was made with all the information executing on it now will get you closer to the truth.
